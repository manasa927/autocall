01 — User Research

Meet Emily

Emily Parker
Primary User Persona
20 years old
College Student
Drives for work, school & life
Limited car knowledge
Parents handle car decisions

Dependency

Emily relies entirely on her parents for anything car-related. When they're unavailable, she's completely stuck with no fallback.

"What If" Anxiety

She constantly worries about potential car problems — not because they're happening, but because she wouldn't know how to handle them if they did.

Phone Call Dread

Calling a mechanic is a source of genuine anxiety. Technical language, fear of being taken advantage of, and uncertainty about what to say all compound.

No Navigation Tools

When something goes wrong, she doesn't even know where to begin — which mechanic, what to say, or whether the issue is urgent enough to act on.

"My dad usually does everything for my car. I wouldn't even know who to call."

02 — Problem Definition

The Breaking Moment

Three scenarios consistently triggered the anxiety that defines the user experience gap:

Warning Light

A light appears on the dashboard. Urgent? Ignorable? Without context, every warning light triggers a spiral of anxiety and inaction.

Unfamiliar Noise

The car starts making a strange sound. She knows something is wrong but has no vocabulary to describe it, no idea who to call, and no confidence to start.

Maintenance Alert

A notification pops up for scheduled maintenance. She's never made this call alone before and freezes at the thought of having to navigate the conversation.

Many people feel anxious and confused when talking to mechanics. They struggle to understand technical terms, ask the right questions, and feel confident making decisions. Current solutions focus on pricing and booking — but don't support users during real conversations, leaving a gap in confidence and clarity.

03 — Design Process

14 Weeks of Iteration

Our solution evolved significantly from its original concept. Each iteration taught us something critical.

Iteration 01

Co-Owner Accounts

Our first approach tackled dependency by sharing the burden — allowing a co-owner (parent, partner, friend) to be linked to the account and notified of issues. Reducing individual responsibility by distributing it.

Insight: Users still needed to know what to say in the moment
Iteration 02

Conversation Scripts ("What to Say" Cards)

Instead of sharing the problem, we gave users the language to handle it themselves. Pre-written scripts and quick-select cards that could be read aloud or referenced during a call with a mechanic.

Insight: Scripts were static — they couldn't adapt to what the mechanic said back
Iteration 03 — Testing & Pivot

Contextual Booking Scripts

We integrated issue-specific scripts directly into the booking flow. The app surfaced better scripts based on what the user was reporting — a brakes issue got brake-specific language, an oil alert got service language. Better, but still couldn't help users when mechanics responded unexpectedly.

Pivot: The real problem is the live conversation, not the prep before it
Iteration 04 — Final Innovation

AutoCall Proxy — AI-Powered Live Call Assistance

The breakthrough: instead of preparing users for calls, we built a system that actively participates in them. The AI can either handle the entire call autonomously, or act as a live co-pilot during the user's own conversation — listening, transcribing, and suggesting responses in real time.

Final Solution: Give them a conversation, not information
04 — Final Solution

AutoCall Proxy

Two modes. One mission: make every driver feel confident enough to handle their car independently.

Feature 01

AI Speaks

The AI handles the entire call with the mechanic on the user's behalf. The user provides context — the AI does the talking, asks the right questions, and reports back in plain language.

  • Fully autonomous call handling
  • Designed for high-anxiety and speech-impaired users
  • Bilingual support
  • Plain-language summary delivered after the call
Feature 02

AI Prompts

The AI listens live and surfaces suggested responses the user can choose from. They can either say the response themselves or tap to have the AI speak it — full control over how much they want to lean on the system.

  • Real-time response suggestions
  • User speaks or AI speaks — their choice
  • Live transcript of the full conversation
  • Technical terms explained inline
Feature 03

Text Assist

Everything AutoCall does on a call, Text Assist does in your messages app. Users who prefer texting mechanics — or need to follow up after a call — get the same AI support, just in written form.

  • Same AI support as the call modes, via SMS
  • Drafts messages for users to review and send
  • Helps users who prefer text communication
  • Works with any messaging platform
Feature 04

Explain My Issue

Before any call or text, users can describe what they're experiencing in plain everyday language. The AI translates it into the right automotive vocabulary — so users walk into every interaction already knowing what to say.

  • Plain-language issue input
  • Translated into mechanic-ready terminology
  • Helps users understand urgency level
  • Prepares users before they reach out

While most apps focus on the car, we focus on the person.

07 — Reflections

What We Learned

01

Design Is Iterative

Our solution evolved significantly from its initial concept and continues to be refined. Staying open-minded and adaptable throughout development isn't a soft skill — it's the core skill.

02

Focus Beats Features

We initially tried to solve everything. Narrowing our scope reduced cognitive overload for users and made the app dramatically more intuitive and effective.

03

Balance User Needs with Market Reality

Learning to evaluate both user demand and broader market adoption trends helped us make more informed decisions about which features to pursue and which to cut.

04

The Emotional Layer Is the Problem

Technical problems have technical solutions. But user hesitation rooted in anxiety requires a fundamentally different approach — one that meets the user emotionally before meeting them functionally.

08 — Looking Ahead

Future Plans

Planned improvements to make AutoCall more effective, more intuitive, and more independence-enabling:

Enhanced Accessibility Features

Making the interface easier to navigate for all users, with a focus on screen reader compatibility and reduced cognitive load design.

In-App Scheduling

Allowing users to book maintenance or services directly without relying on third-party platforms — closing the loop from discovery to action.

Smart Maintenance Notifications

Alerting users about upcoming repairs and routine checkups to support more proactive and independent decision-making before problems arise.
